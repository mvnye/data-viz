How are voter frequency, income category, and education level related?

Those making $125k or more with a college education make up the most of both of those who sporadically vote and those who always vote. Those making less than $40k with a high school or less education make up the most of those who rarely or never vote. Additionally, this figure suggests that the higher one's education level, the higher one's income.


Absentee vs. Turnout Voters from 2014 to 2022

All years have a relatively low absentee voter turnout except for 2020, most likely because of the COVID-19 pandemic. Many people did not have access to in-person voting booths and had to use mail-in ballots. The two presidential election years, 2016 and 2020, generally high a higher percentage of voter turnout than the other years.


2022 Voter Turnout in Virginia

The southwestern localities have the lowest percentage of voter turnout. Most of the counties with the highest voter turnout are to the North and East of Virginia.
